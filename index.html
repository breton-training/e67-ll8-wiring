CAN Low (GMLAN−) X3-33 TN · 0.35mm² · 2501 CAN-L OBDII pin 14 → MPVI4
Sensor low references — leave floating for bench flash. X1-9 (BK·2759) · X1-36 (BN·1271) · X1-37 (PU·1272) · X3-21 (BK·2751) · X3-35 (BK·2761). These are ECM-sourced sensor returns, only active when sensors are physically wired. Connect to the ground bus during full engine harness build, not now.
OBDII CAN routing — two separate wires, terminator at OBDII end only. Run X3-53 and X3-33 as two independent wires all the way to OBDII Pin 6 and Pin 14 respectively. Do not bridge or join them at the ECM side. The 120 Ω terminator sits across Pin 6 ↔ Pin 14 at the OBDII connector only.
✓ Factory donor pigtail (2008 Trailblazer)
120 Ω terminator already built in — taped union between Pin 6 and Pin 14 inside the harness. This donor measured 121 Ω (within 1% of nominal). No external resistor needed. Confirm with a meter before assuming on a different vehicle's donor pigtail.
⚠ Aftermarket / new OBDII socket + pigtail
No built-in terminator. You must install an external 120 Ω resistor across OBDII Pin 6 ↔ Pin 14 or HP Tuners will report "no module response." Build with 0.5 W 1% resistors — for example 10 + 10 + 100 Ω in series = 120 Ω exactly. Solder inline on Pin 6 or Pin 14 wire at the OBDII connector end and insulate.
Verify with meter before powering the ECM. Expected reading at OBDII Pin 6 ↔ Pin 14: 120 Ω with ECM disconnected · ~60 Ω with ECM connected (two 120 Ω terminators in parallel — one in pigtail, one inside the ECM).
OBDII other required pins: Pin 4 (chassis GND) and Pin 5 (signal GND) → bench ground bus. Pin 16 (battery 12 V) → bench supply (+) — powers the MPVI4. Pin 2 (Class 2 / VATS) is not needed for bench flash. Full pinout in systems view.
